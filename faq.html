Q: What does the P/E ratio, beta, and EPS mean?

P/E Ratio (Price to Earnings ratio): The ratio of a company's share price to the company's earnings per share (EPS). A high P/E ratio could mean a stock's price is high compared to its earnings possibly meaning the company is overvalued. For example, Tesla's p/e ratio is 1,265, indicating that the company is more than likely overvalued.

Beta: A measure of a stock's volatility in relation to the market. If a beta value is over 1, it's more volatile compared to the market and if it's below 1, it's less volatile compared to the market. For example, a beta value of 1.12 means the share is 12% more volatile compared to the market.

Earnings per Share (EPS): A company's profit divided by the outstanding shares of its common stock.

Q: What's the difference between an IPO and a SPAC?

An IPO (Initial Public Offering) is when a company works to raise money by issuing shares to the public. A SPAC (Special Purpose Acquisition Company) raises money for the purpose of acquiring another company. It's basically a company with no business trying to acquire a business to make money.
